# FZGM Large-Scale Throughput Experiment — 2026-06-30

the steady-state GB/s numbers and the effective VRAM ceiling for single-call
compression. Cross-cuts four pipeline variants to isolate the impact of memory
strategy (`PREALLOCATE` vs `MINIMAL`) and outlier-buffer size on the ceiling.

## Memory ceiling analysis

### Summary table

| Pipeline | Strategy | outlier_cap | Max successful benchmark | Max successful single-shot compress |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cusz | PREALLOCATE | 0.20 | **4 GB** | 8 GB (compress+decomp single-shot OK; benchmark OOM) |
| fzgpu | PREALLOCATE | 0.20 | **4 GB** | **≥16 GB** |
| cusz_minimal | MINIMAL | 0.20 | **4 GB** | 8 GB |
| cusz_minimal_lowoutlier | MINIMAL | 0.05 | **8 GB** | 8 GB |

Single-shot compress/decompress and in-process benchmark (`-b`) have different ceilings
because PREALLOCATE holds both compress AND decompress buffers simultaneously at init.

### Where each ceiling comes from

**cusz 8 GB benchmark OOM** — error: *"Pool allocation for decompress output failed
(8000000000 bytes); pool may be exhausted."* PREALLOCATE reserved the full compress-phase
pool (input 8 GB + quant codes 4 GB + outlier buffer 3.2 GB + Huffman output ~4 GB ≈ 19 GB)
then failed trying to add the 8 GB decompress output buffer on top.

**cusz_minimal 8 GB benchmark OOM** — error: *"Failed to allocate buffer:
Huffman_to_LorenzoQuant."* MINIMAL did not help here because the Huffman output
buffer (worst-case N × 2 bytes = 4 GB at 8 GB input) is allocated by the Huffman stage
itself regardless of strategy. The pipeline buffer between stages consumed the remaining
pool before the decompress output could be allocated.

**cusz_minimal_lowoutlier 8 GB benchmark OK** — reducing `outlier_capacity` from 0.20 to
0.05 freed ~2.4 GB (= 0.15 × 2B elements × 8 bytes/outlier). Combined with MINIMAL, this
created enough headroom for the pipeline buffer + decompress output. Result: **first
successful 8 GB in-process benchmark** (93.874 ms, 85.0 GB/s compress, 69.6 GB/s decomp).

**cusz 12 GB compress OOM** (both PREALLOCATE and MINIMAL) — error: *"LorenzoQuantStage
kernel launch failed: out of memory."* At 12 GB input the Huffman worst-case output buffer
alone is 6 GB (N × uint16). Combined with 12 GB input + 6 GB quant codes + 4.8 GB
outlier buffer, the compress-phase total likely exceeds 40 GB with CUDA context overhead.
MINIMAL does not help because the Huffman stage's output buffer is not controlled by the
pipeline strategy — it is a fixed worst-case allocation internal to HuffmanStage.

**fzgpu 12/16 GB compress OK, benchmark OOM** — BitplaneRZE's compressed output is tiny
(CR ~194 means ~41 MB for a 8 GB input). No large Huffman output buffer is needed, so
the compress-phase footprint stays manageable even at 16 GB. The benchmark still OOMs
at 8 GB because PREALLOCATE holds both phases' buffers simultaneously; the BitplaneRZE
kernel itself fails: *"bitplane_rze_kernels.cu:56 — cudaGetLastError() → out of memory."*

### Predicted next step: `fzgpu + MINIMAL`

Not yet run. Prediction: MINIMAL eliminates the pre-allocation of decompress buffers
during the compress phase. Since BitplaneRZE has no large output buffer, the benchmark
should fit at 8 GB and very likely at 12 GB as well. This variant is the most promising
path toward a high VRAM ceiling for both single-shot and benchmarked throughput.

## Key findings

1. **Throughput is stable from 1–4 GB at ~80–90 GB/s compress for cusz, ~130–155 GB/s
   for fzgpu.** No meaningful degradation with size — FZGM is bandwidth-saturated across
   this range. The 0.5 GB cells are slightly faster (L2 cache effects across the 5 reps).

2. **fzgpu (BitplaneRZE) outperforms cusz (Huffman) on both metrics simultaneously:**
   - CR: ~194 vs ~30 on scientific data (6.5× better)
   - Compress throughput: 130–153 vs 80–112 GB/s (~40–70% faster)
   - Decompress throughput: 112–136 vs 69–74 GB/s (~60–85% faster)
   - The CR gap reflects BitplaneRZE's efficiency on the highly correlated (Lorenzo-
     predicted) residuals from scientific data; HACC (low compressibility) reverses the
     CR advantage (5.27 vs 6.32) since BitplaneRZE has no Huffman entropy stage.

3. **Real-data anchors validate the synthetic sweep.** NYX (0.537 GB) matches the
   synth_0p5GB cell within ~7% on all metrics. HACC shows expected divergence (lower CR,
   lower TP) consistent with its low-compressibility particle data.

4. **MINIMAL alone does not extend the ceiling.** cusz_minimal has identical failure
   points to cusz_PREALLOCATE. The Huffman output buffer is the binding constraint at
   12 GB, and neither strategy eliminates it.

5. **MINIMAL + outlier_capacity=0.05 unlocks 8 GB benchmarks for Huffman-based
   pipelines.** Throughput at 8 GB (85.0 GB/s compress) continues the flat trend from
   4 GB (86.0 GB/s) — no performance penalty for going to 8 GB.

6. **For Huffman pipelines the 12 GB compress ceiling appears hard** without a library
   change to the Huffman output buffer (stream-encode rather than worst-case pre-allocate).

7. **fzgpu + MINIMAL is the predicted path to ≥12 GB benchmarks.** BitplaneRZE already
   handles ≥16 GB single-shot; MINIMAL should allow benchmark mode to follow.
